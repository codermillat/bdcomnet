# BDComNet Law Enforcement Cooperation Policy

**Version:** 1.0 (Draft)
**Organization:** Bangladesh Community Disaster Communication Society (to be registered)
**Legal Contact:** [lawyer@bdcomnet.org.bd — to be filled after registration]

---

## Principle

BDComNet operates as neutral communication infrastructure — identical in legal standing to a telephone company or postal service. We do not monitor, produce, or control the content of communications on our network. We cooperate fully with valid legal process.

---

## What We Can Provide (With Valid Court Order)

The following metadata logs are retained for **90 days** and are accessible with a valid court order from a competent Bangladesh court:

| Data Type | Available |
|---|---|
| Which node IDs were active at what times | ✅ |
| Which node IDs communicated with which others | ✅ |
| When connections were made and their duration | ✅ |
| Geographic location of fixed tower nodes | ✅ |

---

## What We Cannot Provide (Technically Impossible)

The following data is end-to-end encrypted. Encryption keys exist only on sender and receiver devices. The network operator does not hold decryption keys and cannot provide this data under any circumstances — this is a technical impossibility, not a policy choice:

| Data Type | Available |
|---|---|
| Content of any messages | ❌ Encrypted |
| Content of any voice calls | ❌ Encrypted |
| Files or images transferred | ❌ Encrypted |

This is the same encryption model used by WhatsApp, Signal, and all major banking applications.

---

## Request Process

```
Step 1: Law enforcement sends formal written request
        to: legal@bdcomnet.org.bd

Step 2: Our registered lawyer reviews within 48 hours
        for legal validity and scope

Step 3: Valid court order exists
        → Provide available metadata logs for specified
          node IDs and timeframe only

Step 4: No valid court order
        → Formal written response explaining required
          documentation and legal process

Step 5: All requests and responses logged and published
        in annual transparency report
```

---

## What Is Prohibited on This Network

Users who agree to our Terms of Service acknowledge the following is strictly prohibited:

- Planning or coordinating criminal activity
- Terrorism or incitement to violence
- Child sexual abuse material (CSAM)
- Fraud or financial crimes
- Any activity illegal under Bangladesh law

Users are fully responsible for their own communications. BDComNet is neutral infrastructure — we are not the publisher of user content.

---

## Annual Transparency Report

BDComNet publishes an annual transparency report disclosing:
- Number of law enforcement requests received
- Number of requests complied with (with valid court order)
- Number of requests rejected (insufficient legal basis)
- Types of data provided

This report is published publicly at bdcomnet.org.bd/transparency.

---

*This policy follows the common carrier model established by telecommunications law and is consistent with the cooperative framework used by Guifi.net, NYC Mesh, and major telecommunications operators worldwide.*
